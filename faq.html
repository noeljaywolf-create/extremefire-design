Fire Protection Systems

What fire protection standards do you design to?

We design all systems in accordance with NFPA 13 (Sprinkler Systems), NFPA 72 (Fire Alarm Code), EN 12845 (Fixed Firefighting Systems), ASIB guidelines, and local Zimbabwean authority requirements. Our designs meet international and local code compliance for commercial, industrial, and mining facilities.

Do you provide fire sprinkler system design and hydraulic calculations?

Yes. Our core service includes complete fire sprinkler system design with computerized hydraulic calculations using industry-standard software. We provide fully dimensioned layouts, pipe sizing, pressure loss calculations, pump sizing, and material schedules — all stamped by qualified fire protection engineers.

What types of fire suppression systems do you install?

We work with wet pipe, dry pipe, pre-action, deluge, foam, FM-200, CO₂, and inert gas systems. We also install and maintain fire hydrant systems, hose reel systems, and special hazard suppression for server rooms, generator rooms, and hazardous material storage.

What is the difference between a fire alarm and fire detection system?

Fire detection systems (smoke/heat detectors) sense fire and signal the control panel. The fire alarm system includes detection plus notification appliances (sounders, strobes, voice evacuation), control functions (door release, HVAC shutdown, lift recall), and communication to monitoring stations. We design and install both as integrated systems.

What is a fire pump and when is one required?

A fire pump boosts water pressure to meet sprinkler/standpipe demand when municipal supply is insufficient. Required when hydraulic calculations show pressure deficit, building height exceeds municipal capability, or for high-hazard storage. We design, supply, install, and commission electric and diesel fire pump sets per NFPA 20.

How do I know if my building needs a fire sprinkler system?

Requirements depend on occupancy type, building height, floor area, and local bylaws. In Zimbabwe, sprinklers are mandated for high-rise buildings (>30m), large warehouses/factories, shopping centres, hospitals, hotels, underground parking, and hazardous occupancies. We offer free preliminary consultations to determine your obligations.

Compliance & Standards

How often should fire protection systems be inspected?

Per NFPA 25 and local regulations: sprinkler systems require quarterly, semi-annual, and annual inspections; fire pumps need weekly/monthly/annual testing; fire alarm systems require annual testing per NFPA 72; extinguishers need monthly visual checks and annual professional maintenance. We provide scheduled maintenance contracts.

What is involved in a fire risk assessment?

Our assessment evaluates: fire hazards and ignition sources, people at risk, existing protection measures, means of escape, detection and warning systems, firefighting equipment, management procedures, and training. We deliver a detailed report with prioritized recommendations for compliance and risk reduction.

Can you help with fire safety compliance for insurance?

Yes. We work with insurers and brokers to ensure fire protection meets policy conditions: risk surveys, COPE reports, sprinkler impairment management, pump testing certificates, alarm certification, and extinguisher compliance. Our documentation is accepted by major insurers in Zimbabwe and the region.

What is the process for getting a system approved?

1) Engage us for design. 2) We prepare drawings & calculations per NFPA/ASIB/local codes. 3) Submit to local authority for plan approval. 4) Install per approved plans. 5) Coordinate testing & commissioning with authority witness. 6) Obtain compliance/occupancy certificate. We manage the full process end-to-end.

Do you provide as-built drawings and documentation?

Yes. Every project includes as-built drawings (AutoCAD), hydraulic calculation reports, material certificates, pump test curves, alarm zone charts, operation & maintenance manuals, and commissioning certificates — all organized in a digital handover package for building owners and authorities.

How do you handle system impairments (shutdowns)?

We follow NFPA 25 impairment procedures: 1) Notify insurance, fire brigade, and building management. 2) Implement fire watch if required. 3) Isolate only affected zone. 4) Expedite repairs. 5) Restore, test, and document. 6) Notify all parties. Our impairment management reduces downtime and maintains insurance coverage.
