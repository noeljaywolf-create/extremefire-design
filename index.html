Fire Protection Design

Our fire protection design services provide complete, accurate, and professionally engineered drawings that meet the requirements of reviewers, stocklisters, installers, and project stakeholders.

Our design packages include:

  • Fully dimensioned fire protection layouts
  • Hydraulic calculation reports
  • Fire sprinkler system designs
  • Main and branch line identification
  • Material schedules and cut lengths
  • Hanger locations and installation details
  • Code-compliant engineering documentation

Every design is developed to maximize system performance, improve efficiency, and ensure compliance with applicable fire protection standards.

Hydraulic Calculations for Fire Protection Systems

Hydraulic calculations are a critical component of fire protection engineering. Extreme Fire Design utilizes advanced hydraulic calculation software to ensure every fire protection system is accurately designed for real-world fire scenarios.

Hydraulic calculations help determine the required water flow, pressure, pipe sizing, and system performance needed to achieve effective fire suppression.

Benefits of Hydraulic Calculations

  • Improved design accuracy
  • Optimized water flow and pressure requirements
  • Cost-effective system sizing
  • Enhanced system reliability
  • Reduced energy consumption
  • Compliance with fire protection standards

By preventing both over-design and under-design, hydraulic calculations ensure systems deliver maximum performance while controlling installation and operational costs.

Compliance with International Fire Protection Standards

All fire protection systems designed by Extreme Fire Design are developed in accordance with recognized industry standards and regulations, including:

  • NFPA 13 - Standard for the Installation of Sprinkler Systems
  • EN 12845 - Fixed Firefighting Systems Standards
  • ASIB Standards and Guidelines
  • Local Authority and Regulatory Requirements

Compliance with these standards helps ensure safety, reliability, legal compliance, and long-term system effectiveness.
